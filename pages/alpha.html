What is Alpha?

Alpha is an international initiative that creates a space where anyone can explore faith, ask questions, and discuss life's biggest topics in a welcoming, non-judgmental environment. Originally created by Holy Trinity Brompton Church in London in the 1970s, Alpha has been used by millions of people worldwide to encounter Christ and grow in faith.

At St. Peter's, Alpha is designed for anyone—whether you're a lifelong Catholic looking to deepen your faith, someone curious about Christianity, or someone exploring spirituality for the first time. There's no pressure, no judgment, and no charge.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Alpha really free?

Yes! Alpha is completely free to attend. We provide dinner each week as part of the program at no cost to you. The goal is to remove barriers so that anyone who wants to explore faith can do so.

Do I need to know anything about Christianity to attend?

Not at all! Alpha is designed for people of all backgrounds and levels of faith—from those new to Christianity to longtime Catholics. There's no expectation of prior knowledge. All questions are welcome and encouraged.

Can I bring a friend?

Absolutely! Bring friends, family members, or anyone you think might be interested in exploring faith. Alpha is a wonderful opportunity to share your faith journey with others and discover together.

What if I miss a session?

It's okay if you miss a session! While consistency helps you get the most out of Alpha, we understand that life happens. Each session stands somewhat on its own, and the small group community is forgiving and supportive.

Is there pressure to commit to anything?

No. Alpha is pressure-free. You're free to come and go as you're able, ask questions without judgment, and take the experience at your own pace. There's no expectation to agree with anything or make commitments.
