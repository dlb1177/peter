Perpetual Adoration

"Could you not watch with me one hour?" Matthew 26:40

In Eucharistic Adoration we come before Jesus Christ, truly present in the Blessed Sacrament, to worship Him, to rest in His presence, and to pour out our hearts. At St. Peter's this devotion never stops — our parish keeps watch around the clock, every day of the year.

A Parish That Never Stops Praying

Perpetual Adoration is an outward sign of our parish's commitment to the adoration of Jesus Christ in the Holy Eucharist. Day and night, members of our community unite in taking hours of adoration in the Chapel of the Nativity — so that, at every moment, someone is here keeping watch with the Lord.

Adorers commit to a regular weekly hour — you can sign up for a perpetual adoration time online — but everyone is welcome to stop in to pray at any time. Whether you have a full hour or a few quiet minutes, you will find a peaceful place to bring your joys, your worries, and your intentions to Jesus.

Many who begin with a single hour describe it as the most peaceful and grounding part of their week — a standing appointment with the One who loves them most.

New to Adoration?

There is no special method required — simply come and be with the Lord. You might pray with Scripture, pray the Rosary, read, journal, or simply sit in silence. The most important thing is to show up; Jesus does the rest.
