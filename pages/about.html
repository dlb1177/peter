About St. Peter

The Church of St. Peter is the oldest parish in Minnesota, with roots reaching back to 1840. For nearly 200 years, we have served as a beacon of faith and community in Mendota.

Our History

In 1840, Father Lucien Galtier established the Church of St. Peter in Mendota, Minnesota—marking the beginning of the oldest parish in the state. In the earliest days, Father Galtier celebrated the first Masses in a small log chapel built in 1841. The faith community that gathered there laid the spiritual foundation that continues to sustain our parish today.

Twelve years later, in 1853, the parish constructed the beautiful historic stone church that still stands on our grounds today. This remarkable building is one of the oldest churches in Minnesota and remains a testament to the enduring faith and craftsmanship of our early parish members. The historic church is used for daily Masses and continues to inspire all who enter its sacred space.

Historic Significance

The Church of St. Peter holds tremendous historical significance for Minnesota and the Catholic Church. Not only is it Minnesota's oldest parish, but Mendota itself is the oldest permanent settlement in Minnesota. The historic stone church building is recognized on the National Register of Historic Places, honoring its architectural and historical value.

Our heritage is a source of deep pride and inspiration. The faith of our founders, the sacrifice of our ancestors, and the dedication of countless parishioners through nearly two centuries have shaped who we are today. We honor this legacy by remaining faithful to the Gospel and responsive to the needs of our community.

Mendota: Minnesota's Oldest Settlement

Mendota, nestled at the confluence of the Minnesota and Mississippi Rivers, has the distinction of being Minnesota's oldest permanent European settlement. Long before Minnesota became a state, Mendota served as a vital trading post and community hub. The Church of St. Peter emerged from this rich history, serving not only as a place of worship but as a cornerstone of community life.

Today, our parish continues the legacy of faith and community that has characterized Mendota for nearly two centuries. We remain committed to welcoming all who seek a community of faith, serving our neighbors with compassion, and keeping alive the spiritual traditions that have sustained our people through generations.
